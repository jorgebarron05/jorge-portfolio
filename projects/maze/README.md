As follows is the documentation for our game:

------------------------------------------------------------------------
Part 1
-- We have a fully-working part 1 (building and drawing random mazes) that is thoroughly tested.
-- We have multiple big-bang examples (or makeScenes) that you can uncomment
to run different maze sizes. 
** You can also click the 'R' key 
once you run the big-bang to reset the maze and see that it works 
(extra feature for designing new mazes efficiently)

------------------------------------------------------------------------
Part 2 (EXTRA CREDIT):

-- We have made an attempt at the breadth-first search and depth-first search.
** Press 'B' and 'D' respectively to see it in action.

-- We have also made the fully interactive up, down, right, 
left arrow keys user interaction that prevents illegal moves by the player. The game will end once you have reached the final cell in the maze (distinguished by a red color) thereby notifying the player of completion of the game. 
** Press ←, →, ↑, and ↓ to move the player cells.
** A win message along with your number of steps will be displayed as well when the scene ends.
(although it might take a little long for the message to show up-- it will show!)

-- We have also added a convenience constructor that creates horizontally 
and vertically biased mazes.
** See this in action by uncommenting the labeled big bang tests (using custom biased examples)
we have included in our ExamplesMazeWorld class.

Thanks for playing! :)
